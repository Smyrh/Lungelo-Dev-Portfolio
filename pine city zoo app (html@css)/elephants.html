ELEPHANT

Elephants are the biggest terrestrial animals , they are found mostly in dense forests and are always found in groups.

Three living species are currently recognised: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant.

The elephants are black in colour with a small tail, two big ears, thick legs, a trunk, and two little eyes. The teeth of elephants are very costly and are referred to as tusks.

They are found most often in savannas, grasslands, and forests, but they occupy a wide range of habitats, including deserts, swamps, and highlands in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia
